FPS

What is FPS?

FPS stands for "Frames Per Second". It measures how many frames (images) your computer can render and display every second while playing a game. Higher FPS means smoother gameplay, as your screen refreshes images more frequently.

Note that all FPS performance data presented are theoretical and may vary in real-world usage. The FPS data is based on third-party testing conducted by UL and is provided for reference purposes only. Actual performance may differ.

What should my FPS be for a good gaming experience?

  • 30 FPS: Playable but may feel choppy, especially in fast-paced games.
  • 60 FPS: Ideal for most games, providing smooth and responsive gameplay.
  • 120 FPS or higher: Excellent for competitive gaming, offering a fluid experience on high-refresh-rate monitors.

PS: If you have a monitor with higher resolution (e.g., 1440p), you might need a stronger GPU to reach enough FPS for good gaming experience.

How to increase my FPS?

The most effective way to increase your FPS is by upgrading your GPU, CPU, and RAM, as these are the key hardware components that affect gaming performance. Additionally, adjusting the in-game graphics settings—such as lowering resolution, shadows, and texture quality—can also help improve FPS to a certain extent, depending on your computer’s capabilities.

What does the 3DMark score mean?

The 3DMark score is a benchmark score that measures the performance of a computer’s graphics hardware, primarily for gaming. The higher the score, the stronger performance.

Level 1: Entry Gaming

Provides a basic 1080p gaming experience at low settings—ideal for light or older titles.

Level 2: Intermediate Gaming

Delivers smooth 1080p gameplay at medium to high settings in mainstream titles.

Level 3: High-Performance Gaming

Capable of high settings at 1440p or ultra settings at 1080p in modern games.

Level 4: Top-Tier Gaming

Designed for ultra settings at 1440p or high settings at 4K in demanding AAA titles.

Level 5: Elite Tier Gaming

Provides the ultimate experience at 4K ultra settings with the highest frame rates available.
